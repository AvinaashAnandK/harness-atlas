A model is not an agent.
The harness makes it operational.

Treat the model as a broadly capable new hire: strong at general reasoning, unfamiliar with your systems, policies, and history. A harness is the scaffolding that helps it orient itself, act safely, carry state forward, and improve after failure.

The mental model

A harness does four jobs on every run.

  1. OrientSupply the right world and approach.
  2. ActControl what can touch real systems.
  3. RememberRestore the relevant history.
  4. ImproveTurn failures into reusable fixes.

Limitations and guardrails cut across the loop: some protect the operator, some protect the builder, and some are hard ceilings.

A lens, not a law

Which harness investments survive a better model?

The fate labels are investment hypotheses. Visible, measurable failures may need less bespoke scaffolding as models improve. Silent failures, enforcement boundaries, and business constraints tend to persist.

CompoundsSilent failure; the encoded fix keeps its value.
May fadeVisible failure; bespoke scaffolding may shrink.

The takeaway

The roadmap question is not “How smart is the model?”

Ask where a failure would surface, what the blast radius is, and whether a better model removes the need or merely makes the failure harder to notice.
