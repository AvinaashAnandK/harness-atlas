An atlas of agentic harnesses

The Harness Atlas

In 2026 the moat is not the model. It is the scaffolding around it. And not all scaffolding is equal: some compounds, some decays, and some is there to protect the builder, not to make the agent better.

What this is

This is my attempt at an atlas of agentic harnesses: the capabilities every serious harness ends up building, the real failures that force each one into existence, and which of these investments last.

It is organized the way the territory is organized. Five layers, from how an agent gets oriented to the hard walls around it. Twenty-four capabilities, each one a question the agent has to answer. Three fates, because scaffolding ages in three different ways, and telling them apart is the entire game.

The living atlas

The atlas starts with a teardown of one specimen: Claude Code, opened up by the March 31, 2026 source-map leak (about 1,900 files and 512,000 lines) and the wave of releases that followed it. From there it grows. Capabilities observed in other harnesses keep getting mapped in: Cursor, Replit, OpenClaw, Shopify's agents, Intercom Fin, Google's agent stack, and whatever ships next.

Claude Code is the first specimen, not the subject.

5 layers· 24 capabilities· 140+ documented incidents

Every failure in this atlas is a real, cited incident. No hypotheticals. Where a claim could not be verified against a primary source, the atlas says so.
